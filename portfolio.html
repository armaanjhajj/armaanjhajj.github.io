medicine

i have been an MA (Medical Assistant), EMR (Emergency Medical Responder), and EMT (Emergency Medical Technician).

as a Medical Assistant at private practices, i was in charge of acting as a liaison between the patient and doctor. as patients entered, i simply prepped them for their visit by taking preliminary vitals including weight, height, hearing, and vision tests, and making sure the doctor or PA on shift received all pertinent information before meeting with them. as an MA, i was also in charge of most if not all office manager roles. i had to retrieve patient labs and prep back for visiting patients and prep new visit sheets, create new patient files for new patients, acquire all insurance information from patients for billing, and contact insurance companies to ensure active plans when entering information to our billing platform. i was in charge of issuing/charging visit co-pays and even billing ICD-10 codes from doctors' visitation notes. i would then send all collected samples out to lab/testing companies like LabCorp and Quest. i was finally in charge of upkeeping all other office responsibilities like cleaning and taking out trash.

as an EMR (Emergency Medical Responder) at OnTime Ambulance in Roselle, NJ:

  • Patient care: Administering CPR, controlling bleeding, stabilizing injuries, and providing basic first aid and oxygen therapy
  • Scene assessment: Evaluating the safety of the emergency scene, protecting patients from further harm, and summoning appropriate assistance
  • Patient stabilization: Providing immediate care to keep a patient stable until paramedics or EMTs arrive
  • Transport: Transporting patients to facilities or assisting with their transport from the scene
  • Assistance: Helping higher-level medical staff, such as EMTs and paramedics, with their duties
  • Equipment and vehicle maintenance: Ensuring emergency equipment is ready and performing vehicle checks, cleaning, and fueling if the role involves driving an ambulance

i acquired my EMT licensure from Robert Wood Johnson Barnabas and now am EMT certified and licensed in the state of New Jersey.
